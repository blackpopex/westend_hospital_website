Welcome to Department of Otolaryngology

Otolaryngology, more commonly known as ENT, addresses problems such as frequent ear infections, deviated septums in the nostrils, tonsillitis, persistent sinus infections or sinus pain, and swallowing disorders. It is actually the oldest medical specialty in the United States. Our specialists also treat life-threatening issues that require their advanced training and years of expertise. Tumors of the head and neck, as well as injuries to the face, necessitate a special level of skills for which our team is trained.

Otolaryngology (ENT) At A Glance

Ear Problems

Our providers commonly see ear infections. When ear infections keep recurring, there may be a structural issue that needs to be corrected surgically. We are rated Number one (1) in ENT surgeries.

Throat Problems

Tonsillitis is another common issue for many patients. In addition to performing tonsillectomies for this ailment, our specialists treat individuals with vocal cord disorders and swallowing disorders.

Nose Problems

Recurrent sinus infections that cause pain can be successfully corrected. In addition to sinus infections, we treat patients whose deviated septums cause one of their nostrils to be larger than the other.

Otolaryngology deals with disorders of the ear, nose and throat, which include the head and neck. At the Department of Otolaryngology, we are on the cutting-edge of diagnosing, treating and rehabilitating patients with diseases of the head and neck. And, in coordination with our Comprehensive Cancer Center, we expertly treat head and neck cancers.

  • General otolaryngology (common ear, nose and throat complaints)
  • Cochlear implant.
  • Diseases of the ear.
  • Hearing loss.
  • Nose and sinus disorders.
  • Thyroid and parathyroid disorders.
  • Sleep disorders.
  • Facial and skull trauma.
  • Facial paralysis (facial movement disorders).
  • Acoustic neuromas (tumors of the balance nerve that affect hearing).
