Welcome to Pharmacy Department

The Westend Hospital Pharmacy is so much more than just a place where drugs are prescribed, we provide a wide range of services to our patients, staff, and visitors. We offer a variety of prescriptions and over-the-counter medications as well as medical supplies and equipment. We are here to answer your questions and help you get the most out of your medication.

Our dispensary is a place where top-quality drugs are dispensed.

Offering both Inpatient and Outpatient services

For patients of Westend Hospital, the Pharmacy Department supervises the selection and use of medications. To ensure that all medications are prescribed appropriately, given safely, and have the intended effect, we work closely with the medical doctors and nursing personnel.

In-patient services.

Services and products that are often provided by a hospital for the care and treatment of patients that are admitted into the hospital are known as inpatient hospital services. These services must be offered under the supervision of a doctor at a facility with a license that is principally dedicated to the treatment and care of various disease conditions.

We also give medical professionals advice on prescribing choices and patient monitoring during hospital stays. During doctor rounds, our pharmacists frequently encounter patients and answer inquiries regarding their medications, drug allergies, etc.

Out-patient services.

This is a service or treatment that does not necessitate an overnight stay.

There are different levels of care that a person may receive when they need medical assistance, whether it is for a catastrophic accident, an unexpected illness, or a routine check-up. Due to different locales' potential for greater specialization in one sort of care over another, certain settings are more ideal than others. One of the most typical contexts for medical care is outpatient care, where a variety of tasks or levels of care can be carried out.

We work hard to ensure a seamless return to your house. To guarantee that you have the prescription you require before leaving the hospital, our team takes care of all essential details, including obtaining permission from your insurance company. We also spend time educating you about your prescription to make sure you adhere to the recommended drug regimen.

Your safety is important to us.

A pharmacy's safety procedures are effective. Team members are devoted to sharing knowledge to improve the quality of life of the patients. Members of the pharmacy team have duties and responsibilities that are well-defined. Additionally, the pharmacy effectively manages the risks involved in assigning its work by conducting frequent audits.

Applicable standards

  • Pharmacy team members are encouraged to broaden their knowledge and abilities via the pharmacy's effective learning and development techniques. Members of the pharmacy team who operate in specialized roles aimed at improving the outcomes of patients.
  • Pharmacy services are frequently evaluated and regulated for quality and safety by regulatory bodies.
  • Staff members with clearly defined duties and lines of accountability offer pharmacy services.
  • The employees are competent and have the necessary training and credentials for their position and the duties they perform.
  • Staff members are allowed to apply their professional judgment in the best interests of patients while adhering to their own legal and professional obligations.

The Pharmacy Department has about 2 pharmacists, together with about 4 technicians and support staff.

In an environment like ours, where we provide care for patients taking several drugs for co-morbidity, a broad spectrum of clinical experience is very helpful hence our pharmacists and technicians are licensed.

In this context, technology is extremely important. We can increase the safety of drug usage by efficient prescribing, monitoring, use of computerized prescriber order entry, and other technology-driven initiatives.
